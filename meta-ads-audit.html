The Core Problem

The account is trending in the wrong direction. Prior 30d, 14 Mar to 12 Apr delivered 55 subs at $104 CPA. Last 30d delivered 30 subs at $179 CPA. Purchase volume halved while CPA jumped 73%. The 7-day window confirms it: $1,059 spent, only 6 conversions, CPA $177.

Three Things Going Wrong At Once

1. Creative fatigue. Frequency on Broad climbed to 2.99 (30d) on the same hero ads
2. Lookalike is broken. $314 spent last 7 days for zero conversions
3. Pixel data integrity. 100% of PageView events are flagged for invalid emails, hurting CAPI-driven targeting

Where The Upside Is

This is a salvageable account with decent fundamentals: clear winning creative (Avoids Dangerous Crosswinds at $111 CPR over 90d, 39 conversions on $4,348), a defensible product (Navy-meteorologist credibility, no real competitors), and a high-retention subscription model where 2y LTV likely exceeds $150. Fix the pixel, refresh creative, tidy up the ad-set structure and a sustainable CPA below $100 at scaled spend is realistic inside 60 days.

Methodology & Caveats

Data Sources & What this audit covers and what it doesn't

What's in the dataset

  • Meta Ads Manager CSV exports for 5 rolling windows: 90d, 60d, 30d, 14d, 7d
  • Campaign, ad set, and ad-level views (spend, impressions, reach, results, cost-per-result, quality ranking)
  • Funnel events from Events Manager (PageView, Lead, Purchase, SureRoute Annual)
  • Audience inventory from Audiences Manager
  • Live ad-set targeting screenshots (Broad & Lookalike Stacked)
  • Holiday landing page screenshot (rvweather.com/landing-page-holiday-2025)

What's missing or limited

  • No purchase_value column in the CSV exports, so ROAS is derived from the $99.99 annual sub price (verified on rvweather.com)
  • No CTR, CPC, hook rate or thru-play data in export, creative analysis is based on cost-per-result and quality ranking only
  • Pixel match quality issue (100% of PageView events with invalid emails) means Meta-attributed numbers likely under-count vs Shopify/Stripe truth
  • Subscription LTV modelled on industry-typical 50% Y2 renewal, 70% Y3-of-Y2, needs Stripe data to confirm
  • Monthly subs ($19.99/mo) not in scope of this optimisation event, they're additional upside

What this changes

Every ROAS and unit-economics number in this deck is a model, not a measurement. First action when I take over is wiring up purchase_value tracking on the SureRoute Annual event and reconciling against Stripe weekly. Until that's done, treat CPA as the primary KPI (it's directly measured) and treat ROAS as directional.

Account Architecture

Targeting & Structure Review

What's well-built

  • Single scaling campaign (HM | Scaling), clean CBO setup using ad-set-level budgets
  • Advantage+ audience enabled across all sets, gives Meta room to optimise
  • Sensible geo: US + Canada with AK/HI excluded (avoiding low-converting fringe geos)
  • Age 25-65+, appropriate for RV demo skew older
  • Custom Audiences for retargeting/exclusion exist (5 lookalikes ready)
  • Strong custom event setup: SureRoute Annual as primary conversion (Match Quality 7.7/10)

What's broken or stale

  • 26 ads in the Lookalike Stacked ad set, way over Meta's recommended 4-6 active variants per ad set, fragmenting learning
  • 21 ads in Broad, same problem
  • No retargeting ad set for trial-starters or PageView-but-not-purchase
  • 3 customer-list audiences (RVWx Daily, Starter Tier, Membership) locked behind "Accept terms", sitting unused since May 2024
  • No exclusion of existing customers from prospecting sets
  • The "Broad Homepage" ad set has never spent, wasted real estate
  • Lookalikes built on 180d window when 730d is now available for purchase events (per Meta's May 2026 update)

Take

The account is set up as a prospecting-only machine. There's no retargeting layer, no funnel logic, and the prospecting ad sets are creatively cluttered. The 26-ad Lookalike set means Meta can only meaningfully optimise to 4-5 of those creatives. The other 20+ are dilution. The fix is structural before it's creative.

CRO Audit

Holiday 2025 Landing Page Review

What's strong

  • Hero promises specific benefit: "drive smarter, travel safer"
  • SureRoute is the named hero product, clear positioning
  • Dave Titley credibility built in (Navy meteorologist, 50 years experience)
  • Pricing transparent: $99.99 annual, $19.99 monthly
  • 30-day money-back guarantee de-risks the purchase
  • Testimonials/reviews layered through the page
  • Free 7-day trial removes top-of-funnel friction

What's hurting conversion

  • Page is a holiday-themed URL being run in May, temporal mismatch erodes trust
  • Slow above-the-fold: hero requires scrolling before primary CTA appears clearly
  • No scarcity, urgency or risk-reversal in the headline
  • Pricing > conversion-rate-killer shown before perceived value built
  • No video sales letter (VSL) at top, text-heavy
  • Pixel sending invalid emails. The page is breaking CAPI at form level
  • No exit-intent capture visible
  • Ad creative says "Built by a Navy meteorologist" but the landing page doesn't lead with this, so there's a message-match miss

Conversion math

Last 28 days, account-wide

PageViews 43,800
Leads (trial signups) 210 (0.48%)
Purchases 122
Trial-to-paid 58%

Where the drop-off is

Trial-to-paid is strong at 58% (benchmark 15-30%); once someone signs up for the trial, the product does the selling.

The leak is PageView to Lead at 0.48%. If we move that to even a conservative 1.5%, leads triple to ~650/month and (at 58% trial-to-paid) purchases triple to ~375/month, at the same ad spend.

The biggest single fix

Landing page CRO is the highest-ROI fix in this account. We're scaling on a leaky bucket. Every dollar in ad optimisation works 3x harder once LP conversion is fixed.
